\n                                            And watch young people dance the jarocho\r\nWearing T-shirts that say \"Undocumented\"\n                                        \n                                        \n                                        \n                                        Surrounded by a sea of brown faces\r\nA celebration,\r\nAn affirmation of self, of dignity,\r\nSurrounded by history,\r\nAs a young man on the simplest wooden platform\r\nTips his baseball cap with elegant respect\r\nAnd the rhythm of tapping feet pronounces\r\nThat the longer the struggle\r\nThe more precious the legacy of pride handed down.\r\n \r\nPersecution is ever present\r\nRaids and racism mark our lives\r\nAnd change is far away.\r\n \r\nAnd yet we live, we march, we dance\r\nNot huddled in the shadows as some would say\r\nBut making our way into the bright sunlight\r\nLike a young shoot pushing through the heavy soil.\r\nThe author, a poet, co-chairs the Latino/Latina Roundtable of the San Gabriel and Pomona Valley Immigration Committee.\r\nRead more: Opinion poetry by Times readers\r\n\n                                    \n                                \n                                \n
